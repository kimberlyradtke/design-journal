Asymmetry

What is it?

Asymmetrical balance is another way to provide organize content and create focus within a web page, however it has to be used well and effectively. Asymmetrical designs are balanced, however they are not two identical halves. An example might be the use of an image to balance out large typography. Asymmetrical design can shift the eye back and forth between the balancing elements. It is important that these elements have a commonality and relate to each other in order for it to make sense.

Why I chose it?

I chose Cake Sweet Cake to showcase the effective use of asymmetrical balance. The first thing that the eye is drawn to is the large cake image on the right hand side. The size of this cake might be overwhelming, however it is complimented with the use of appropriately sized and styled typography. The use of descriptive text and the cake image clearly shows what the site is about at first glance. It is is great way to incorporate descriptive words and a visual element.
