WHITE SPACE

What is it?

White space in a design is the empty space between other elements. This can include the space in between lines of text, the space around graphics, and margins and padding for instance. If used effectively, white space helps the viewers eye navigate around the page with ease. It also can emphasize objects and cause the eye to focus in certain areas. White space does not have to actually be white in color. It depends on the colors used in the design.

Why I chose it?

I chose eighty3creative to showcase effective use of white space. This site uses white space to set up boundaries which allows the eye to focus on important sections of the page. The mission statement of this page is surrounded by white space which draws my eye to it. This is important because the statement is sitting under a large banner which can be an attention stealer. The fact that there is a clean chunk of white space directly underneath helps to pull the focus away from the banner and down to the mission statement.
